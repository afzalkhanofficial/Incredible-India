Seasonal Travel Guide

India's climate varies from tropical in the south to temperate in the north. Choose the perfect season for your adventure.

ac_unit Dec - Feb

Winter

Pleasant and dry across most of India. Perfect for desert safaris and exploring heritage sites comfortably.

backpack What to Pack
  • check_circle Heavy woolens for North
  • check_circle Light layers for South
  • check_circle Moisturizer & lip balm
Best Places:

Rajasthan, Kerala, Goa, Delhi

sunny Mar - May

Summer

Temperatures rise. Ideal time to escape to the cool Himalayas or spot tigers in national parks as water holes dry up.

backpack What to Pack
  • check_circle Breathable cottons
  • check_circle Sunblock & sunglasses
  • check_circle Wide-brimmed hats
Best Places:

Himachal, Ladakh, Kashmir, Sikkim

rainy Jun - Sep

Monsoon

Lush green landscapes and rejuvenating rains. Great for Ayurveda retreats and witnessing nature's abundance.

backpack What to Pack
  • check_circle Waterproof gear
  • check_circle Quick-dry fabrics
  • check_circle Umbrella & rain boots
Best Places:

Western Ghats, Kerala, Valley of Flowers

eco Oct - Nov

Post-Monsoon

Clear skies and moderate temperatures return. The festive season begins, making it a vibrant time to visit.

backpack What to Pack
  • check_circle Light woolens
  • check_circle Comfortable walking shoes
  • check_circle Ethnic wear for festivals
Best Places:

Golden Triangle, Varanasi, Mysore

Travel Essentials

Quick answers for your journey.

Most foreign nationals need a visa. The e-Visa is available for tourists from over 160 countries. Apply at least 4 days before travel.

October to March is generally the best time to visit India. The weather is pleasant and perfect for sightseeing across most regions.

The Indian Rupee (INR/₹) is the official currency. Major credit cards are widely accepted in cities. ATMs are readily available throughout the country.
