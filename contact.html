Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about traveling to India.

Most foreign nationals need a visa to enter India. The e-Visa is available for tourists from over 160 countries and can be obtained online. Types include Tourist e-Visa (30 days, 1 year, or 5 years), Business e-Visa, and Medical e-Visa. Apply at least 4 days before your travel date through the official e-Visa portal: indianvisaonline.gov.in

The best time depends on the region. Generally, October to March is ideal for most of India due to pleasant weather. The Himalayas are best visited in summer (April-June), typically creating a cooler escape. Monsoon season (July-September) offers lush greenery, especially in Kerala and Goa.

Yes, India is generally safe for tourists. Like any destination, standard precautions are advised: safeguard valuables, use reputable transport, and respect local customs. The dedicated Tourist Police force operates in many major cities to assist travelers.

The currency is the Indian Rupee (INR). ATMs are widely available in cities and towns. International credit and debit cards are accepted in most hotels, restaurants, and shops in urban areas, but carrying some cash is recommended for smaller vendors and rural areas.

India has 22 official languages, with Hindi and English being the official languages of the Union Government. English is widely spoken and understood in major cities, tourist hubs, and hotels, making communication relatively easy for international travelers.

India uses Type C, D, and M plugs. The standard voltage is 230V and the standard frequency is 50Hz. Travelers are advised to bring a universal travel adapter.
